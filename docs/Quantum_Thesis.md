# Quantum & Crypto: Bet on the Migration, Not the Doomsday

*An early-stage investment thesis · 2-page · June 2026*

## The thesis

Quantum computing will not end crypto. It will force the largest cryptographic migration in its history — and that migration, not the doomsday, is the trade. Over the next three to five years the durable opportunity for an early-stage investor is not wagering on *if or when* a cryptographically relevant quantum computer (CRQC) arrives. That is a hardware coin-flip we cannot underwrite. The opportunity is the **migration layer**: the signatures, wallets, agility tooling and proofs that must carry several trillion dollars of value and the base chains themselves onto quantum-safe foundations while keeping trust intact.

This sits squarely in how we already think about crypto. Crypto exists to turn "trust me" into "verify it." Quantum doesn't just threaten encryption — it threatens the *verifiable commitments* that are crypto's entire reason to exist. Whoever rebuilds those commitments on post-quantum foundations owns critical infrastructure for the decade after. The winners won't be the loudest about Q-Day; they'll be the teams whose rails everyone is forced to migrate onto.

## Why now — the gap between a closing threat and a long migration

The threat compressed sharply in 2026. In March, Google Quantum AI estimated that breaking 256-bit elliptic-curve cryptography — what Bitcoin and Ethereum use to sign — could require roughly 1,200 logical qubits, about 20× fewer than prior estimates. No CRQC exists today, and the largest machines (IBM's ~120-qubit Nighthawk) are still generations away; credible Q-Day estimates range from ~2028 to the early 2030s. The point is not the exact date. The point is the **gap** between an uncertain Q-Day and a migration that takes years — Bitcoin's own proposed sunset spans roughly five, Ethereum targets core post-quantum infrastructure by ~2029.

Two things make this investable now rather than later. First, "harvest-now, decrypt-later": exposed public keys are recordable today and breakable afterward — over a third of all Bitcoin already sits in quantum-exposed addresses. The clock started before the computer exists. Second, the migration has visibly begun: Bitcoin merged **BIP-360** (a quantum-resistant address type) in February 2026 and proposed **BIP-361**, sunsetting legacy signatures, in April; Ethereum stood up a dedicated **post-quantum security team** in January 2026, betting on account abstraction for signature agility and hash-based signatures for validators. The edge is to invest in the gap — after the threat stopped being science fiction, before the migration is fully priced.

## Categories worth backing (3–5 years)

**1. Crypto-agility middleware — highest conviction.** The transition *is* the product. Almost every chain, wallet, custodian and protocol hard-codes a single signature scheme; swapping it without forks or downtime is largely unsolved. Tooling that lets systems rotate schemes, dual-sign through the transition, and audit quantum exposure is the picks-and-shovels layer for a migration the whole industry is obligated to undertake. The TLS/SSL migration minted durable infrastructure businesses; this is larger and has a hard deadline.

**2. Quantum-safe wallets, signing & account abstraction.** The end-user surface and the likeliest land-grab. Account abstraction (Ethereum's EIP-8141 path) turns signature agility into a product feature — users move to ML-DSA / FN-DSA or hash-based schemes without changing addresses. Quantum-safe-by-default wallets, signers and custody, plus the key-rotation UX that makes migration painless, win the holders who are *forced* to act. Solana's Winternitz vault and Ethereum's leanXMSS validator path are early signals of demand.

**3. Harvest-now-decrypt-later mitigation & exposure intelligence — the near-term revenue.** The clock is already running, so the first real budgets are defensive. Tools that quantify an entity's quantum exposure (reused addresses, exposed public keys, mempool leakage) and proactively migrate or shield value serve funds, custodians, exchanges and treasuries with capital at risk *today* — not in 2030.

**4. Post-quantum × zero-knowledge — the deep-tech, thesis-aligned bet.** ZK is core crypto infrastructure, and much of it (and the curves it rests on) must be made quantum-safe; hash-based and lattice proof systems are an open frontier. The talent here is exactly our hunting ground — cryptographers — and the primitives enable new mechanisms, such as the zero-knowledge proof-of-seed recovery Bitcoin is researching to rescue frozen coins. Longer horizon, highest defensibility.

**5. Quantum-safe L1s/L2s — watch, mostly premature.** New quantum-native chains are the obvious bet and, for this window, usually the wrong one. The incumbents are migrating *in place*, so a new L1 must win distribution it doesn't have, on a threat that isn't yet acute. Investable only where a chain earns a real application or community first and quantum-safety is a feature rather than the pitch.

## What we'd avoid

We avoid the two crowded mistakes: quantum-doom narratives selling fear, and timing bets on hardware. Underwriting whether a CRQC lands in 2028 versus 2034 is speculation we can't win, and a thesis that needs a specific Q-Day to pay off is fragile. We also avoid betting *against* the incumbents' in-place migration — Bitcoin and Ethereum are moving, so the money is in the rails that serve that migration, not in replacing them. The durable value is in tooling that gets used regardless of the exact date the threat arrives.

## Conclusion

When the cryptography breaks, trust has to be rebuilt — and crypto is the trust layer. The teams that build the migration's rails (agility, quantum-safe signing, exposure intelligence, post-quantum-secure proofs) compound value through the transition and beyond. The doomsayers and the date-bettors do not. Bet on the migration.
